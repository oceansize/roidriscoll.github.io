The Trouble With Javascript

written Jun 23rd, 2014

…is that it’s ugly as sin. A face only a Mother could love. It looks like it fell out of the ugly tree, and hit every branch on the way down. Unfortunately for me, it also seems to be an essential part of being a Web Developer, so I guess I’ll just have to suck it up.

Part of it is that for the last couple of months I’ve been swimming in the tranquil lakes of Ruby – one of the most elegant languages on the planet. But when you see how javascript can enhance a webpage it quickly becomes irrelevant how crap one has to wade through: if you want to engage with your visitors via the browser, you need javascript.

The first real difference to strike me was the catch-all term function(). According to our tutor Alex, there are three main uses for this particular piece of syntax: * To define a Class * Define a Method * Take the place of ‘do & end’ (as would be seen in Ruby)
